First Aid Essentials

  • Keep a well-stocked first aid kit at home and in your vehicle.
  • Ensure hands are clean before giving or receiving first aid.
  • Stay calm and assess the situation before acting.
  • Call for medical help if the injury appears serious. Dial 911
  • Learn basic CPR and wound care techniques.

Common Injuries and Solutions

Dog Bite

1. Wash the bite area with soap and water thoroughly.

2. Apply antiseptic like iodine or alcohol.

3. Cover with a clean bandage.

4. Seek immediate medical attention or rabies vaccination.

Cuts and Scrapes

1. Stop any bleeding by applying gentle pressure with a sterile cloth.

2. Clean the wound with water and mild soap.

3. Apply antiseptic and cover with a sterile bandage.

4. Change the dressing daily and watch for signs of infection.

3. Cover with a sterile gauze.

4. If severe, seek immediate medical help.

Nosebleed

1. Sit and lean forward slightly.

2. Pinch the nose just below the bridge for 10 minutes.

3. Avoid tilting your head back or lying down.

4. If bleeding continues beyond 20 minutes, contact a doctor.

Sprain

1. Rest the injured area and avoid movement.

2. Apply ice wrapped in cloth for 15-20 minutes.

3. Compress with an elastic bandage and elevate the limb.

4. If swelling or pain persists, seek medical advice.

Emergency Delivery

1. Have towels or blankets ready to dry & warm the baby. Make sure the mother is lying down or propped up to prevent injury from a fall. Also, wash your hands thoroughly before touching the mother or baby.

2. When the head appears, support the perineum (the area between the vagina and anus) with a hand while gently guiding the baby's head.

3.Once the head is out, check if the umbilical cord is around the baby's neck. If it is, gently try to unloop it or loosen it enough for the body to slip through.

4. With the next contraction, the shoulders should emerge. You may need to apply gentle downward traction for the anterior shoulder and guide the body upward to deliver the posterior shoulder.

5. Do not pull/cut on the umbilical cord at this stage.

6. Dry the baby with a clean, warm towel and stimulate them to help them breathe. Place the baby on the mother's chest.

7. Stay with the mother until the placenta delivers, which may take a few minutes. Do not pull on the cord.
